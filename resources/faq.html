What should I do first when I arrive at a property?

‚ñº

Follow these steps in order:

  • Check-in via App: Mark your arrival in the Qbees app
  • Initial Inspection: Walk through the property and take before photos

Procedures

What's the difference between Check-in and Check-out cleans?

‚ñº

Check-in Clean: Preparing property for arriving guests

  • Coordinate ETA with guest
  • Full deep clean of all areas
  • Fresh linen setup (sometimes done by linen team)
  • Consumables restocked
  • Welcome setup (kitchen essentials visible)

Check-out Clean: Cleaning after guests depart

  • Strip all used linen and towels
  • Remove all rubbish and check bins
  • Full property clean and inspection
  • Note any damages or missing items
  • Take comprehensive photos
Procedures

What is an Initial Clean and how is it different?

‚ñº

An Initial Clean is for properties that haven't been professionally cleaned before or need extra attention. Key differences:

  • Extra time allocated: +50% more time than standard clean
  • Deep cleaning focus: Behind appliances, inside cupboards, baseboards
  • Higher standards: Bring property up to Qbees quality level
  • Extensive photos: Document before/after condition
  • Premium rate: Charged at 1.5x standard rate

Think of it as establishing a baseline of cleanliness that future cleans will maintain.

Cleaning

What order should I clean rooms in?

‚ñº

Recommended cleaning order:

  1. Bathroom: Most time-consuming, allows chemicals to work while you clean other areas
  2. Kitchen: Second most intensive area
  3. Bedrooms: Change linen, dust, vacuum
  4. Living Areas: Dust, wipe, vacuum
  5. Laundry: Usually quick to clean
  6. Final touches: Floors, entry areas, final inspection

Pro Tip: Spray bathroom surfaces with cleaner first, then move to another room while chemicals break down soap scum and grime!

Cleaning

How do I know if I need to do a Deep Clean vs Normal Clean?

‚ñº

Normal Clean (Standard):

  • Visible surfaces only
  • Regular maintenance level
  • Allocated time is base rate

Deep Clean (When specified):

  • Inside cupboards and drawers
  • Behind and under appliances
  • Baseboards and window sills
  • Light fixtures and ceiling fans
  • Grout scrubbing
  • +50% time allocated

The job type will be specified in your assignment. When in doubt, check with your property manager!

Cleaning

What are the most common cleaning mistakes to avoid?

‚ñº

Top mistakes and how to avoid them:

  • Skipping pre-spray: Always spray surfaces and let chemicals work before wiping
  • Using wrong chemicals: Follow the chemical guide - wrong products damage surfaces
  • Forgetting touch points: Light switches, door handles, remotes - these are critical!
  • Missing inside/outside: Both sides of shower doors, fridge doors, mirrors
  • Rushing photos: Poor photos mean you have to come back
  • Not checking all rooms: Always do final walk-through before checkout
  • Leaving streaks: Buff glass and mirrors with dry cloth after cleaning
Chemicals

Which chemical should I use for each area?

‚ñº

Quick reference guide:

  • Lencia (Blue): Bathrooms, showers, mould, tiles
  • Taski (Yellow): General surfaces, bench tops, tables
  • Halo (Green): Glass, mirrors, windows
  • Magic (Pink): Kitchens, ovens, stovetops, grease
  • Shelley (White): Stainless steel appliances, taps
  • Ajax (Red): Tough stains, toilets, heavy-duty cleaning

Safety Reminder: Lencia, Magic, and Ajax MUST be diluted with water. Always read labels before use!

For full details, see the Chemical Guide

What's the microfibre cloth color system?

‚ñº

Microfibre cloths are color-coded to prevent cross-contamination:

  • Blue: Bathrooms (showers, tubs, sinks)
  • Yellow: General surfaces (dusting, benchtops)
  • Green: Glass and mirrors
  • Red: Kitchens (stovetops, ovens, grease)
  • White: Stainless steel and polishing

Critical Rule: Never mix bathroom and kitchen cloths! This prevents spreading bacteria and ensures hygiene standards.

Chemicals

How do I dilute chemicals safely?

‚ñº

Dilution Guidelines:

  • Lencia: 1 part Lencia : 3 parts water (25% solution)
  • Magic: 1 part Magic : 10 parts water (10% solution)
  • Ajax: Follow bottle instructions, usually light sprinkle in water

Safety Steps:

  1. Always add chemical to water (never water to chemical)
  2. Use spray bottles with clear labels
  3. Test on small area first if unsure
  4. Never mix different chemicals together
  5. Wear gloves when handling concentrated chemicals

Warning: Undiluted chemicals can damage surfaces and are unsafe to use directly!

How is cleaning time calculated?

‚ñº

Base time is calculated by property configuration:

  • 1 bed, 1 bath apartment: 2.0 hours
  • 2 bed, 2 bath apartment: 3.0 hours
  • 3 bed, 2 bath house: 4.0 hours

Time adjustments:

  • +0.25 hours per extra bathroom
  • +1.5x for Deep Clean or Initial Clean
  • Houses get +0.25 hours vs apartments

Communication

What should I report to the property manager?

‚ñº

Always report:

  • Missing Items: Towels, consumables, furniture, keys

Photos

What photos are required for every clean?

‚ñº

Mandatory photos for all cleans:

Photos

Why are my photos being rejected?

‚ñº

Common reasons for photo rejection:

  • Too dark: Open curtains and turn on lights
  • Blurry: Hold phone steady, clean camera lens
  • Too close: Step back to show full area
  • Wrong angle: Portrait instead of landscape
  • Missing areas: Didn't capture what was requested
  • Cluttered: Your cleaning supplies in the shot
  • Poor composition: Important areas cut off

How to take perfect photos:

  1. Review the photo checklist in Module 4
  2. Take photos from room corners for wide angles
  3. Check each photo before moving to next room
  4. Retake immediately if quality is poor
  5. Remove personal items from frame (cleaning caddy, vacuum)
Procedures

What should I do if I can't finish in the allocated time?

‚ñº

First, assess why:

  • Is the property significantly messier than expected?
  • Are there unexpected issues (heavy stains, extra rooms)?
  • Did you need to wait for access?

Then take action:

  1. Document with photos: Show the extra work required
  2. Contact property manager: Explain situation and estimated extra time
  3. Get approval: Wait for confirmation before continuing
  4. Complete the work: Once approved, finish to standard
  5. Note in app: Record actual time and reason for variance

Prevention Tips:

  • Work efficiently - follow cleaning order in Module 2
  • Don't spend too long on one area
  • Use pre-spray technique to save time
  • Practice makes you faster - timing improves with experience
Procedures

What do I do if I find damaged or broken items?

‚ñº

Immediate Actions:

  1. Stop and document: Take multiple clear photos of damage
  2. Note details: What's damaged, where, how bad
  3. Check your before photos: Was it there when you arrived?
  4. Report via app: Send photos and description to property manager
  5. Don't attempt repairs: Unless specifically instructed

Important: This is why before photos are critical! They protect you from false damage claims.

For Safety Hazards:

  • Broken glass: Clean up carefully, document, report
  • Water leaks: Turn off water if possible, report immediately
  • Electrical issues: Don't touch, call property manager
  • Gas smells: Evacuate, call emergency services first, then property manager
Procedures

How do I handle rejected linen?

‚ñº

What qualifies as rejected linen:

  • Tears or holes
  • Permanent stains
  • Bleach marks or discoloration
  • Worn fabric (thin, see-through)
  • Damaged zippers or buttons

Rejection Process:

  1. Identify: Check all linen as you strip beds
  2. Photograph: Take clear photo of damage with item laid flat
  3. Bag separately: Put in different bag from regular dirty linen
  4. Label: Mark bag "REJECTED LINEN - [Property Name]"
  5. Report: Upload photo to app with description
  6. Return: Follow linen team instructions for pickup

Never: Throw away rejected linen without documentation and approval!
